Chapter 2 of Adventurers is all about Professions, and contains the templates
nearly all players will use to build their characters. Your profession is
roughly equivalent to a D&D character's class. It determines the things you are
good at and "how" you're good at them. The ones in this book are:

- **Barbarians** are strong and tough warriors who are adept at wilderness
  survival.

- **Bards** are socially adept characters who can influence minds through words,
  song, and magic.

- **Clerics** channel the divine to heal, bless, and exorcise.

- **Druids** control plants, animals, and the weather. They're also excellent at
  outdoors survival.

- **Holy Warriors** hunt down and slay Evil monsters using divine powers.

- **Knights** are heavily armored warriors trained in personal and group
  tactics.

- **Martial Artists** have mystically-powered athlethic and combat abilities.

- **Scouts** are stealthy survivalists and master archers.

- **Swashbucklers** are roguish skirmishers who rely on speed and skill to
  defend themselves.

- **Thieves** open locks, disarm traps, and excel at both stealth and dungeon
  parkour.

- **Wizards** are masters of weird lore and have the highest variety of spells
  at their disposal.

Each template has a set of starting basic stats, and then "shopping lists" of
Advantages, Disadvantages and Skills. Some of those are given to every new
character, and some are chosen by the player during creation among several
options deemed appropriate for that template. In the end, the player should end
up with a character worth 250 points, with 50 points of disadvantages.

The advantage lists for all templates always include increases to its starting
stats, as well as the Luck advantage which lets them re-roll failed rolls every
once in a while. That's useful to everyone! The rest of the list will be
something thematic to that template, as will the ones for Disadvantages and
Skills.

Personally, I tend to treat the Disadvantage list as more of a suggestion than
as a restriction, and will happily take stuff from outside the template if it's
a better fit for the character I have in mind. As long as it adds up to -50, I'm
good. The Skill lists I tend to use exactly as written, though.

Completing the template is a description of special traits that apply only to
that template, and therefore are described right there instead of in the general
advantage list.
