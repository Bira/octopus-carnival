Hackers are an central element in most cyberpunk RPGs, in exactly the same way
dragons are central to dungeon fantasy: even if one never appears on stage,
just knowing they're out there does a lot to help put you in the right mindset
for the genre.

A lot of cyberpunk RPGs depict the activity in the classic Gibsonian style,
where an extremely specialized hacker PC projects their conscience into a
virtual mini-dungeon and hogs the GM's attention for an extended time. In this
post, I want to try something different, and present rules for hacking in the
style of Ghost in the Shell.

All the shows in this franchise follow a team of badass cyborgs working for a
fictional Japanese government agency. There's no "designated" hacker - all of
the protagonists are competent hackers in addition to whatever their primary
specialty is. Information security is always very important for the plot, but
the show tries to make the act itself as brief as possible on-camera. We either
see the final results of a long intrusion attempt that took hours or days, or a
more or less instant attempt done in support of some other task. The viewer's
attention is firmly kept on the physical plane.

And, of course, the "target system" is often a _person_ instead of a more
traditional server. Every single character in this show has a "cyberbrain", an
implanted computer deeply integrated into their nervous system and equipped with
a wireless connection. It's very useful, but can also leave its users
vulnerable to electronic attacks. The most feared is "ghost hacking", which
messes with their actual organic memories.

Cyberpunk 2077 takes a page from Ghost in the Shell for its own hacking
system. Nearly every character in the game is similarly augmented, and a PC with
hacking abilities can mess with their implants and gear to temporarily turn off
their senses, cause distractions, or even deal direct damage. You don't spend
long sessions in virtual reality crawling through immense networks - rather, you
target individual devices and people with these effects.
