One of the player characters for my [solo Hell's Rebels campaign][2].

Like many dwarves, Urist Stortebeker felt a calling to serve the gods ever since
he came of age. Unlike most of those other dwarves, his calling was to serve the
gods of revelry and freedom, instead of the usual set of dour deities of duty
and grudges. This meant he wasn't a very good fit for the temples back home, and
so he took on the live of a wandering priest.

His wanderings eventually brought him to Kintargo, where he quickly fell in with
the Sacred Order of Archivists, a secret society dedicated to preserving the
true history of Kintargo and all those texts the house of Thrune tried to
extinguish. _They_ worshipped mainly deities of knowledge, but there was enough
of an overlap here that Urist felt right at home. Plus, it was a good cause.

Rather than stay at their headquarters to curate their troves of knowledge, he
instead became a kind of retrieval specialist, traveling far and wide to acquire
rare books and documents. So he got to keep satisfying his wanderlust even after
finding a place to put down roots.

Urist was on one of his away missions when the society was raided by Barzilai
Thrune's forces during the Night of Ashes. He came back to find their hideout
completely taken over by them, and most of the members dead, with their homes
burned. All he had to go on from there was a letter that was given to him by a
couple of friends in the Order before Urist had left - they had an adult son who
might be in danger from Thrune despite not being a member of the order.

So Urist stayed in Kintargo, to find this son and make sure he's all right. And
then he's going to show those diabolist assholes that he's still a dwarf and can
still hold a grudge. And he'll do it while still laughing and celebrating,
because there's nothing a fascist hates more than someone laughing in their
face.

## Description

Urist looks like a very typical fantasy dwarf. Bald, pale skin, blue eyes, white
beard going down to his chest and adorned with braids and a bit of jewelry. He
likes to wear armor even when not preparing for a fight. As a Golarion dwarf, he
carries his clan dagger with him at all times. And as a worshiper of deities
like Caiden Caylean or Avandra, he also carries a mug with him that can serve as
a ritual object or as a handy container for any drinks people might be passing
around.

"Stortebeker" was the epithet of a real-life Dutch pirate and it means something
"overturner of cups", which in turn is a fancy way of saying "someone who drinks
a lot". This feels extremely dwarven to me in a lot of different ways, and has
the bonus of not being a direct reference to violence.

## Build Notes

Urist is a pretty standard DF cleric. He's also a dwarf, though here I'm using
the DFRPG convention of listing his species traits individually with the rest
rather than writing "Dwarf" in the advantages and silently editing the rest of
the sheet.

He's also the "armored and fighty" sort of cleric rather than the "staff and
robes" one, so his future purchases should likely take him further in that
direction, even borrowing stuff from the Holy Warrior archetype when it makes
sense to do so.
