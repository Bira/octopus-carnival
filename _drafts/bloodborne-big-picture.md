When I started this blog way back in 2016, my initial goal was to publish an
adaptation of the Dragon's Dogma setting for GURPS. You can see all that I wrote
about that [right here][1]. I never got around to talking about the endgame
bosses, but the project is otherwise complete.

Now, I'd like to start on another adaptation project, and this time I think I'm
going to pick Bloodborne. The reason is simple, I've been playing it for months
now and finally managed to complete it. I really liked both the setting and
gameplay, and it seems to be that it's well-suited to being adapted to GURPS.

In this introductory post, I'd like to reiterate the stuff I said on [Adapting
Videogames][2]. Bloodborne has a fairly complex system, but we will mainly focus
our attention on its setting elements, and on how to bring them to GURPS in a
way that feels flavorful and reminiscent of the original game. The system bits
work very well for it _as a video game_, but would not necessarily translate
well to the tabletop.

As with the Dragon's Dogma project, we will follow the order of operations
described in [GURPS Adaptations][3]. We'll first work on our High Concept, which
includes what kinds of campaign we expect to support. After that we'll focus on
the setting's geography and history. Then we look at character options, at
equipment, and at monsters.

There's a lot of ground to cover here, and the setting bits in particular will
be a lot harder to figure out than those from the Dragon's Dogma project. From
Software games are infamous for obfuscating their setting information, and this
one is no exception. Most of it is hidden in item descriptions and optional NPC
dialogue. Some of it you have to guess from how certain places or characters
look.

To help me with this effort, I'm going to make use of the excellent [Bloodborne
Wiki][3], the culmination of many heroic efforts by the fan community to
catalogue the game's lore and mechanics. If I run across other sources that
might help with the project, I'll be sure to discuss them too.
