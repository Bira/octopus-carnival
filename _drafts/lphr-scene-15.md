This post is part of [my solo Hell's Rebels campaign][1].

Our intrepid rebels continue their raid of the Many Steps Monastery, aiming to
clear it of its devil-worshiping occupants and score a blow against the fascist
rule of Barzilai Thrune and his bosses who watch from both central Cheliax and
Hell.

Our [previous post][2] was entirely dedicated to preparing the remaining two
fights in this adventure. In this one we'll hopefully get through at least one
of them.

# [Player] Continuing the Delve

Our last position was room F4, and our last turn number was 19.

## Turn 20

The heroes take a passage to map-east, entering a short corridor that has one
door in a wall to their right, and one at the very end, both closed. Urist knows
the side door leads to a lecture hall, and the end door to the common room.

They open the lecture hall door and see that the place is a mess that smells
strongly of sulphur and dog shit. It's probably where Nox's hellhound sleeps,
though it's not here right now. Surely it will be ahead.

They lock this door and proceed to the common room one.

## Turn 21: Combat!

The party still remembers their conversation with Yiliv back at the start of the
raid, so they know there are still a bunch of redactors in here and that they're
all aware the group is coming. There's two chambers left before they clear the
whole place, so they're in for some heavy fighting right about now...

Urist casts a 3-point Armor spell on himself, and the party forms up with Urist
and Arcturus in front and the other three as a second line. Then Art kicks the
door in!

They immediately a pair of burly devils with swords ready to fight among a group
of tables and chairs filled with books and other documents. Just behind them are
three hooded weirdoes. It's a fight!

Art charges right away, aiming to slam into one of the devil guards, but as soon
as he crosses into the room an additional hooded weirdo who had been hidden just
out of sight springs his Readied ambush, stepping forward and attempting to stab
him in the kidneys. His angle is awkward, however, and the knife slides
harmlessly over Art's scale armor as he continues to rush past undeterred.
