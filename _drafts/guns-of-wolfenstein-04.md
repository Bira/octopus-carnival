In [Part 3][1] of this series, we took a look at the guns that appear in
Wolfenstein: The New Order, the second game of the "alternate history" trilogy
by Machine Games. I named its timeline "Wolfenstein-2" for convenience, with
Wolfenstein-1 being the timeline from the 2001 game.

This time, we're looking at the weapons that appear on the 2017 sequel to The
New Order, named Wolfenstein II: The New Colossus. We're still on Wolfenstein-2
for this one, though you might think otherwise from looking at its weapons.

There is one last game in the series, Wolfenstein: Youngblood, which takes place
in 1981, but it features exactly the same weapons as the ones presented in this
post and the previous one. I think it's fair to say that nazi weapon development
completely stagnated after the events of The New Colossus.

## Plot Summary (Contains Spoilers)

We pick up right where we left off in The New Order, though many of the story's
visual and gameplay elements feel significantly different.

B. J. Blazkowicz lies dying on the floor of Deathshead's castle as his allies
prepare to nuke the place. However, those allies manage to mount a rescue before
they pull the trigger, carrying a badly wounded Blazko back to their captured
submarine. He spends the next 5 months in a coma after undergoing extensive
trauma surgery. As in the previous game, he awakens fully in the middle of a
nazi attack.

Frau Engel, who was a secondary antagonist in the previous game, has been
promoted to replace Deathshead. What she lacks in technical skill, she makes up
in petty cruelty. She commands a flying fortress that is currently attacking the
rebel sub. The first level has BJ fighting off this attack while bound to a
wheelchair. He's aided by resistance leader Caroline and her suit of powered
armor, but they end up getting captured and Caroline gets executed before
B. J. manages to put on her powered armor suit and escape with his surviving
allies.

After a brief detour to clear a secret deck on the sub of the Nazis that had
been hiding there and sending transmissions to their bosses, the resistance
decides to follow Caroline's original plan and join together with any surviving
American resistance groups to take the US back and use it as a staging ground to
defeat the Nazis in the rest of the world.

Still wearing the armor, B.J. helps contact and rescue a resistance group from
radioactive New York. Its leader, Grace, ends up filling Caroline's spot in the
group's leadership. He then blows up a Nazi command center built next to ancient
supertech vault under Roswell (yes, there are flying saucers).

As he's leaving, B. J. decides to drive to his childhood home in Mesquite,
Texas, because his mother left a heirloom wedding ring in there and he wants to
use it to propose to Anna. They've been a couple since the last game and she's
expecting twins, so it's about time. However, his racist and abusive father
sells him out to Frau Engel, who arrives in the flying fortress and captures
him.

After many very long and very unpleasant cutscenes, B.J. becomes one of the few
heroic protagonists to survive a beheading, as he's publicly executed and his
head is immediately rescued by a daring rebel plan. Seth joins the head with a
spare supersoldier body (which could be seen in the sub since the start of the
game, as a piece of foreshadowing).

The missions continue after this: rescuing another resistance group from New
Orleans, flying all the way to Venus to recover the password for the flying
fortress' security system, and taking control of it in a spectacular and daring
raid that involves fighting a squad of advanced giant robots. Killing Frau
Engel live on nation-wide TV is just a victory lap after that.

The speech from the resistance leaders that immediately precedes the end credits
was very topical for 2017, and I never thought I'd see it becoming _more_
topical in 2025, but here we are.

## The Guns

You'd think we moved to yet another alternate timeline given how different the
guns in this game work compared to the previous one. The biggest differences are
all due to gameplay changes, so just like in the previous post I'll be trying to
blend them in a way that sounds plausible for a tabletop GURPS game.

A mere 5 months pass between this game and the previous entry in the franchise,
so you could have these guns show up earlier if your campaign is set before the
events of The New Colossus. Alternatively, you could say that some of them are
either from a batch of new developments that was just hitting mass production as
Deathshead died, or were forced into mass adoption when Frau Engel took over
because they were her pet projects.

A final note before we move on to the guns: I'm not bothering with diesel
weapons. They're too silly.

### Pistols

#### Pistol Model 1961

The is almost identical to the **Pistol Model 1960** from the previous article.
The only difference is that it has a sturdier receiver and barrel, which make it
heavier (Weight 1.5kg/0.5kg) but allow it to safely fire "magnum" rounds: Dam 3d
pi, Rcl 3. It can also fire the same standard 9mm rounds as the Model 1960.

You can load magnum rounds on the Model 1960, but that lowers its Malf to 15.

### Long Arms

#### Returning Weapons

This game's upgrade system allows its version of the assault rifle to behave
either like the **Assault Rifle Model 1960** or the **Marksman Rifle 1960** from
the previous post. I recommend you just use those two and keep them as separate
weapons.

It also features a new appearance for the **Shockhammer 1960** with three
rotating barrels, but its stats remain exactly the same. Use whichever "look"
you like best.

#### Machine Pistol 1961

Used extensively by enemies in The New Colossus, this SMG fires a low-caliber
round from a 45-round magazine. It has a retractable wire stock and a
distinctive barrel jacket. If fired one-handed with the stock folded: ST 11, Rcl
3.

The much less common _MP-61S_ has an integral cinematic suppressor that works
like the pistol's, but is identical otherwise.

Both models can take a 90-round drum: -1 to Bulk, Shots 90+1(3), weight becomes
3.55/0.85kg. Both can also fire API ammo: Dam 3d(2) pi- inc.

### Grenades and Launchers

**Tesla Grenades** from the previous post remain in use here, but they're much
less common and share the spotlight with two other grenade types.

#### Explosive Grenade

A standard high-explosive warhead with limited fragmentation in a compact
egg-shaped package. This is the most common grenade as of The New Colossus.

#### Incendiary Grenade

Shaped similarly to the high-explosive variant, this one sprays burning fuel
around the epicenter of its detonation, likely causing fires.

#### Kampfpistole 1960

An evolution of the 1946 model, this one is fed by a bulky cylinder with a 6
round capacity. It fires rockets with a more powerful charge and a less safe
arming distance than its predecessor's grenades. Its rockets are smaller than
the ones fired by the **Underbarrel Rocket Launcher** from the previous post,
but still pack a punch.

This weapon is only issued to certain high-ranking elite officers, more as a
symbol of status than something they're expected to use in combat. It can be
"procured" at any facilities where such officers are posted, either from their
bodies or from storage lockers. It uses Guns (Gyroc) under the standard system,
or Guns (Pistol) and the Payload Warhead technique under the alternate one.

### Heavy Weapons

**Laser Machine Guns** remain popular in The New Colossus, but they're joined by
a few other horrors.

#### Rotary Shotgun

Found in fixed mounts and occasionally wielded by cyborg supersoldiers, this
heavy weapon has 4 rotary barrels and fires 12G shotgun shells at an alarming
rate of fire from a big box drum. With its default load of buckshot, it's of
questionable use against well-protected targets. Its main use is as an
anti-infantry and anti-civilian terror weapon.

Since this fires the same ammo as the Shockhammer, you could conceivably load it
with any special shells that also fit the other weapon. Just like in the game,
though, this is likely to be a "sometimes gun" found in the field and quickly
discarded when it runs dry or when it's no longer practical to lug around. You
use whatever it comes loaded with, and then throw it away.

If using the standard skill system, the weapon is used with either Guns (LMG) or
Gunner (Machine Gun) depending on whether it's mounted or not. With the
alternate system, you want Guns (Long Arm) with the Burst-Fire technique.
