Bloodborne is a fascinating game for many reasons, and one of them is its take
on weapons. The weapons below are nominally TL5, and while they contain no
superscience they're well into Fantasy-Tech territory in conception. They could
be inserted into a Dungeon Fantasy setting without anyone batting an eye, and
more modern versions of them could also find a place in a Monster Hunters
campaign.

The "trick weapons" used by characters in this game tend to have two
forms. Someone familiar with the weapon can switch between forms with a Ready
Action. They might also learn the Fast-Draw (trick weapon) skill, and roll
against it when switching. A success allows them to do so instantly.

In most cases, they're built using the rules for combination gadgets from
**Low-Tech** and **Low-Tech Companion 2**.

## Workshop Weapons

The player character in Bloodborne is carrying on a long tradition of monster
hunting that first evolved from an even older tradition of dungeon delving. Its
first techniques were developed by a group known as the Workshop. Being the
first of its kind, it had no other name.

The Workshop was founded in a time when the most popular and effective cure-all
remedy in their native city of Yarnham was... basically werewolf blood. It was
not yet known that overindulging on "beast blood" could turn someone into a
werewolf, despite it being kinda obvious in retrospect. When this happened, the
hunters of the Workshop were dispatched to deal with the resulting creature.

A perhaps ironic turn of events here is that the hunters themselves made use of
beast blood, to a much greater extent than civilians. They needed the boosted
strength and regenerative abilities such blood confers to stand up to the
beasts. This remains true of all the groups who would later inherit the
Workshop's legacy.

Workshop Hunters labor under a very particular set of constraints. Their weapons
must be able to deal decisive blows to the beasts they hunt, while allowing the
hunters to stay mobile in order to defend themselves. They must also avoid the
eyes of the public, so their equipment must also be discreet.

### Hunter Axe

The most basic of hunter weapons. Hunters who prefer the axe tend to view
themselves as executioners. It's a grim job, but a necessary one, and it should
be done without fuss.

The weapon consists of a one-handed battle axe with a heavy broad head, a small
spear tip, and a haft made entirely of metal. The haft is collapsible, and
extends to turn the weapon into a dueling halberd!

The one-handed axe can only be properly wielded by someone with the enhanced
strength of a Hunter, but it keeps one hand free and might pass for a
wood-cutting tool to a casual observer.  The halberd has greater leverage and
greater reach. It lacks the back spike of a traditional halberd, but that's a
bonus: getting your weapon stuck on a beast means death.

The axe form uses **Axe/Mace**. The halberd form uses **Polearm**. It takes a
Ready maneuver to switch between the two assuming the user already has a hand
free.
