I've been meaning to adapt Bloodborne to GURPS for a while now and I figured I
might as well started here. This is a game that uses a lot of the same "genre
props" as a GURPS Monster Hunters campaign, so its mysterious backstory features
several organizations dedicated to that activity. They're all gone now, but the
gear and techniques they developed remain. Those have a very distinctive
flavor. How would they look like in GURPS?

## The Basics: Tech Level and Genre Switches

Bloodborne's setting is at around TL 5. It's quite possible the Yarnham region
is a bit behind the rest of the world when it comes to visual style, but for
obvious reasons they've been doing plenty of weapon R&D.

Much of that research was done by Hunter Workshops, with the aim of making the
job of a monster hunter easier. Several of these workshops were founded and went
extinct in the decades preceding the game's narrative, and each had their own
ideas about what worked best. The resulting weapons are very visually
distinctive, and their mechanics are clearly cinematic. We are well into Fantasy
Tech territory here.

"Standard" TL 5 or earlier weapons are also available, and you can look at
High-Tech and Low-Tech for those. This post concerns itself with the weird
stuff.

## The Workshop

The Workshop was the first hunter organization founded in Yarnham, attached to
Bringenwerth Academy. It has no other name because it was the only one of its
kind at the time.

I'm guessing it must have begun as a support organization for the academy's tomb
prospectors, who were more of a Dungeon Fantasy lot delving the ancient ruins
beneath present-day Yarnham. When monsters started appearing on the surface due
to the Scourge of the Beast, they refocused towards hunting them down.

Workshop hunters were a small, elite group. They aimed to be both discreet and
effective, quickly eliminating any scourge beasts that appeared before their
existence could become public knowledge. This goal was made harder by the fact
that scourge beasts are often superhumanly strong and tough. This led to the
development of a peculiar combat style, and weapons to support it.

### Workshop Armor

For workshop hunters, the best defense is not getting hit. While some hunters
wear reinforced clothing, mobility and blending into the civilian population
always take precedence over pure protection.

A reinforced hunter outfit counts at most as Light Cloth armor from Dungeon
Fantasy. These _do_ include face protection, both to keep all that blood away
and to hide their identities while the hunt is underway. This would provide DR 1
to all locations except the eyes, weight 18.6 lbs, and cost $153. Most hunters
will refuse to wear even this much armor if it would reduce their Dodge.

### Workshop Guns

Mundane bullets don't work against beasts at all. Workshop guns are modified to
fire "quicksilver" bullets instead. These are made by mixing an alchemical
solution of mercury and other ingredients with a hunter's own blood. Poured into
a mold, the mixture turns into a soft silvery bullet that can be fired from a
specially modified gun.
