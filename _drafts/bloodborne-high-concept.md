In this post, we'll try to figure out the high concept of our adaptation
efforts, and the first necessary step for that is analyzing the original game
itself.

## Bloodborne

Bloodborne is a 2015 game made by a company called From Software, or
FromSoft. FromSoft started out as an office software company, morphed into a
game studio, and really found its niche with Demon's Souls, the first ever game
in the genre that would named after it. Their next title, Dark Souls, would be
an even bigger hit, and would have two sequels.

Bloodborne was released a bit after Dark Souls 2. In a way, it acted as a proof
of concept that this was really a new genre, since it showed that you could vary
the aesthetics and fiddle with the mechanics while still ending up with
something that was recognizably the same type of game. The innovations it
introduced would be refined in future titles, but it's already an excellent game
on its own. Some people call the genre it belongs to "Soulsborne", which hints
at the amount of influence it had. It retains several of the most popular traits
of the genre while also being significantly different from its predecessors.

### Setting

While Dark Souls could be labeled as, well, dark fantasy, Bloodborne has both
feet firmly planted in the horror genre. Its aesthetics move forward from the
medieval to a 18th/19th century look, and its settings are designed with a
gothic veneer that veers more and more into cosmic horror as the player
progresses through the game.
